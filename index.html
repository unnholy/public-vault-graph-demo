What it does

Atlas Fleet parses a bundled sample markdown vault, extracts wiki links and tags, generates a graph, and maps the same notes into an agent-routing dashboard with approval gates and a work queue.

Why it is public-safe

The demo uses synthetic notes. It shows the architecture of a private AI workflow without exposing the private vault, credentials, account details, or personal records.

Architecture

Capture notes enter the vault, the parser turns links into graph edges, the graph exposes hubs and owners, and the queue marks which actions can run automatically versus which require approval.

Privacy

Private content is replaced with sample data. The app does not fetch external data, does not require accounts, and stores nothing remotely.

Build decisions

DecisionReason
Static HTMLEasy to inspect, run, and archive for a hackathon submission.
